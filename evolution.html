Critical Reflection: Evolving
Learning Approaches & Transferable Skills


Approach to Learning: Undergraduate to Postgraduate

My academic journey has traversed diverse terrains, beginning as an undergraduate student majoring in Mass Communication and evolving into a postgraduate pursuing a Master's in Information Technology with a focus on Software Development. Reflecting on this transition, my approach to learning has undergone a significant transformation.

During my undergraduate years, my primary focus was on honing communication skills, understanding media dynamics, and exploring the nuances of mass communication. The learning was often content-centric, involving absorbing facts, theories, and principles. While creativity was encouraged, problem-solving largely revolved around addressing communication challenges within the media landscape.

However, as I transitioned into the postgraduate phase, the shift in emphasis was palpable. The Master's program in Information Technology demanded a more dynamic and multifaceted approach to learning. It extended beyond content consumption to encompass the application of knowledge through hands-on experiences. Concepts of teamwork, complex problem-solving, creative problem-solving, critical thinking, and design thinking became central to my learning process.

Approach to Continuous Learning

This transition instilled in me the profound significance of continuous learning. In the fast-paced realm of technology, the landscape is ever-evolving. To remain relevant and excel in the workplace, a commitment to continuous learning is paramount. My postgraduate experience ingrained in me the understanding that learning is not confined to the classroom but extends into the workplace and beyond.


Transferable Skills: Teamwork and Communication

Two critical transferable skills highlighted in the 2015 Report from the Office of the Chief Scientist are teamwork and communication. Reflecting on my journey, I've witnessed a transformation in how I approach and apply these skills.

Teamwork: During my undergraduate years, teamwork was predominantly within the context of group projects centered on media campaigns or content production. Collaboration was encouraged, but the stakes were often academic.

In contrast, my postgraduate experience brought teamwork to the forefront of professional practice. Working on software development projects demanded seamless collaboration, not only with fellow developers but also with cross-functional teams encompassing designers, project managers, and clients. This experience sharpened my ability to collaborate effectively in diverse and dynamic environments.

Communication: In my undergraduate studies, communication was primarily about conveying messages effectively, often within the confines of media and journalism. It was about crafting compelling narratives and engaging content.

In the postgraduate realm, communication evolved into a multifaceted skill encompassing technical documentation, client communication, and conveying complex technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders. This shift emphasized the importance of adaptability in communication, tailoring my message to suit the audience and context.


Impact on Future Career Success

As I embark on my future career in software development, these evolving approaches to learning and refined transferable skills are pivotal. The workplace demands not only technical expertise but also the ability to collaborate seamlessly in diverse teams and convey technical information comprehensibly to stakeholders.

My commitment to continuous learning positions me to stay abreast of emerging technologies and industry trends. It ensures that I remain adaptable and open to acquiring new skills throughout my career. Moreover, the evolved approaches to teamwork and communication equip me to thrive in the collaborative and dynamic landscape of the IT industry.


In conclusion, my academic evolution from mass communication to information technology has been a transformative journey. It has not only equipped me with technical prowess but also instilled in me a commitment to continuous learning and refined transferable skills critical for success in the workplace. As I look to the future, I embrace these changes as catalysts for my growth and advancement in the ever-evolving world of technology.
