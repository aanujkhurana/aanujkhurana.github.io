Reflection on Ethical Conduct and Professional Accountability


Context: During my placement at Gilmour Space Technologies, I encountered an experience that posed ethical and professional challenges. It revolved around the responsible use of personal information and data.

Experience: I was assigned a task that involved accessing and utilizing personal data from employees within the organization. This data was crucial for the development of certain features in the Chainmail project. However, it raised concerns about privacy, data security, and ethical conduct.

How I Dealt with the Situation:

  1. Data Privacy Awareness: First, I took the initiative to educate myself about data privacy laws and best practices. I familiarized myself with relevant regulations, such as GDPR, to ensure compliance.
  2. Limited Access: I ensured that only authorized team members had access to the personal data. Access controls and permissions were set up to restrict unnecessary exposure.
  3. Data Minimization: I implemented a data minimization approach, only collecting and using the data necessary for the specific task. Unnecessary data was not stored or processed.
  4. Consent and Transparency: I communicated with the employees whose data was involved, explaining why it was necessary and obtaining their consent. Transparency was key in building trust.
  5. Data Security: I took extra precautions to secure the data, using encryption and access controls to protect it from unauthorized access or breaches.
  6. Regular Audits: I conducted regular audits to ensure that data was being used responsibly and that there were no breaches of privacy or data misuse.

What I Did Well:

  • Ethical Awareness: I demonstrated a strong ethical awareness by proactively addressing privacy and data security concerns.
  • Transparency: Open communication and obtaining consent from the individuals involved created a transparent and accountable environment.
  • Security: Implementing robust data security measures helped safeguard the personal information.

What Could Have Been Handled Better:

  • Data Governance: I could have put in place a more structured data governance framework from the beginning to ensure better management of data throughout the project.
  • Privacy Impact Assessment:Conducting a formal privacy impact assessment could have provided a clearer understanding of potential risks and mitigation strategies.

What I Learned:

This experience reinforced the importance of ethical conduct and professional accountability in the ICT industry. It highlighted that responsible data handling should be an integral part of every project. I also learned the significance of seeking continuous improvement in ethical decision-making and data management.


Conclusion, The experience I encountered during my placement at Gilmour Space Technologies emphasized the critical need for ethical conduct and professional accountability, particularly in handling personal data. It reinforced the importance of proactive measures to protect privacy and data security. This experience has significantly shaped my understanding of responsible data usage and will guide my actions in the future as I pursue a career in the ICT industry.
